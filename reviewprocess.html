Review Process

All submitted manuscripts undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process. Here's an overview of the review process:

Initial Evaluation: Upon submission, manuscripts are initially reviewed by the editorial team to ensure they meet the journal's scope and formatting requirements. Submissions that do not meet these criteria may be returned to authors for revision.
Review Reports:Reviewers provide detailed feedback on the manuscript, including strengths, weaknesses, and recommendations for improvement. They also make a recommendation to the editor regarding the manuscript's suitability for publication.
Author Notification: Authors are notified of the editorial decision and, if applicable, provided with the reviewer comments for revision.
Revisions:If revisions are required, authors are given a deadline to submit their revised manuscript along with a detailed response to reviewer comments.
Final Decision:After revisions, the editor-in-chief makes a final decision on the manuscript. If accepted, the manuscript moves to the production stage. Publication:Accepted manuscripts are copyedited, typeset, and published in the journal's upcoming issue.
